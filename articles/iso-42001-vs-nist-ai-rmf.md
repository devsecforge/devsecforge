# ISO/IEC 42001 vs NIST AI RMF: Which AI Governance Framework Fits Your Org

*By S. Naz · Cybersecurity, Cloud & AI Security Leader*

Two frameworks dominate AI governance conversations right now, and they answer slightly different questions. NIST AI RMF asks "how do we manage AI risk well?" ISO/IEC 42001 asks "how do we build and certify a management system for AI?" Understanding that distinction saves months of choosing the wrong starting point.

## NIST AI RMF: Risk-First, US-Rooted, Certification-Optional

NIST AI RMF is a voluntary, risk-management-oriented framework organized around four functions — Govern, Map, Measure, Manage. There's no formal certification; it's designed to be adapted and referenced, not audited against by a third party. It's a strong fit if your primary driver is building a genuinely better internal risk practice, especially for US-headquartered organizations or those selling into US federal or regulated sectors where NIST alignment is often expected informally.

## ISO/IEC 42001: Management-System-First, Certifiable, Globally Recognized

ISO/IEC 42001 is structured like other ISO management system standards (27001, 9001) — it defines an AI Management System (AIMS) with formal requirements, and organizations *can* pursue third-party certification against it. That makes it the stronger choice when you need to demonstrate AI governance maturity to customers, partners, or regulators globally, especially in Europe, where alignment with emerging AI Act obligations matters and a recognizable certification carries real commercial weight.

## They're Not Mutually Exclusive

In practice, the strongest programs use NIST AI RMF's risk functions as the operating model and map that work into ISO 42001's management-system structure for anyone pursuing certification. The risk activities — inventory, assessment, mitigation, monitoring — are largely the same underlying work; the frameworks differ mainly in structure, formality, and whether a certificate comes out the other end.

## How to Choose

If you need a certificate to unlock deals or satisfy a customer's vendor-risk questionnaire, start with ISO 42001. If you need a working internal risk program fast and certification isn't currently a requirement, start with NIST AI RMF and layer ISO structure on later. Either path benefits from the same underlying inventory-and-assessment work described in the NIST AI RMF 90-day plan.

## Key Takeaways

- NIST AI RMF is a risk-management framework; ISO 42001 is a certifiable management-system standard.
- The underlying risk work overlaps heavily — the difference is structure, formality, and certifiability.
- Choose based on whether a third-party certificate is a business requirement, not framework popularity.
